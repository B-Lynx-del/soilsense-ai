## üö® The Problem

### Farmers are Losing Money and Degrading Land

**In Kiambu County:**
- 68% of farmers apply fertilizers without soil testing
- Average farmer wastes **KES 15,000 - 25,000** annually on wrong inputs
- Soil pH has dropped from 6.5 to 5.2 over 20 years (acidification crisis)
- Yields declining by **3-5% annually** despite increased fertilizer use
- 40% of applied fertilizers are wasted due to soil acidity

### Why Existing Solutions Fail

1. **Soil testing is expensive** (KES 3,500 per test at KALRO)
2. **Results are confusing** (technical jargon, no actionable advice)
3. **Recommendations are generic** (not localized to Kiambu)
4. **No cost-benefit analysis** (farmers don't know ROI)
5. **Extension officer shortage** (1 officer per 2,000 farmers)

### The Hidden Cost: Environmental Degradation

- Excess fertilizer ‚Üí water pollution (nitrate runoff into rivers)
- Acidic soils ‚Üí reduced biodiversity
- Poor soil health ‚Üí increased erosion
- Chemical dependency ‚Üí reduced organic matter

## üîÑ How It Works

### For Farmers (3 Simple Steps)

```
1. INPUT DATA
   ‚îî‚îÄ Name, location, farm size, crop
   ‚îî‚îÄ Basic soil test: pH, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium
   ‚îî‚îÄ Observed symptoms (yellowing, poor growth, etc.)

2. AI ANALYSIS (2 minutes)
   ‚îî‚îÄ Analyzes soil chemistry
   ‚îî‚îÄ Identifies deficiencies
   ‚îî‚îÄ Calculates optimal amendments
   ‚îî‚îÄ Estimates costs and ROI

3. GET RECOMMENDATIONS
   ‚îî‚îÄ Exact products needed (lime, fertilizers, manure)
   ‚îî‚îÄ Quantities for your farm size
   ‚îî‚îÄ Where to buy in Kiambu (specific suppliers)
   ‚îî‚îÄ Application instructions & timeline
   ‚îî‚îÄ Expected yield increase (%)
   ‚îî‚îÄ Return on investment calculation
```

### Example User Journey

**Meet John Kamau from Ruiru:**
- **Problem:** Maize yields dropped from 18 bags to 12 bags per acre
- **Input:** pH 5.2, Nitrogen Low, 2 acres, Maize
- **Recommendation:** 
  - 4 tonnes agricultural lime (KES 12,000)
  - 100kg CAN fertilizer (KES 9,500)
  - 10 tonnes manure (KES 8,000)
  - **Total Investment:** KES 29,500
- **Result:** Yield increased to 19 bags per acre (58% increase)
- **ROI:** KES 56,000 additional revenue ‚Üí **90% return**
- **Timeline:** Lime applied in July, planted in October, harvest in February
